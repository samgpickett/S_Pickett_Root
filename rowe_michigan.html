Hotel Rowe

Hotel Rowe opened in 1923.

It was a beautiful building, featuring a grande lobby, 300 rooms, a ballroom, banquet hall, and convention facilities. As depicted on this matchbook is the Hunt Room, a place for dining and dancing. In 1922, the hotel was sold thanks to depts accumulated during the Great Depression. It was sold again in 1940, and two years later it was converted into a temporary domitory for the U.S. Army Air Force Training students. It was released back to the public the following year. Currently, it has been outfitted as a mixed use building.
